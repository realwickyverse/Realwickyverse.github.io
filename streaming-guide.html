⚡ Quick Verdict

Laptop > iPhone for Gaming Streams

Your laptop is significantly better for gaming streams. The RTX 3050 has a built-in NVENC encoder — a dedicated hardware chip for streaming that won't eat into your game performance. Your iPhone is great as a webcam or face cam using Camo or OBS Virtual Camera, but not as the primary streaming device for PC gaming.

02 — Software
OBS SETUP
01

⬇Download & Install

Download OBS Studio (free, obsproject.com). This is the industry standard. Avoid Streamlabs OBS for now — it uses more RAM. Stick with vanilla OBS.

02

🔗Connect YouTube

In OBS → Settings → Stream → Select YouTube - RTMPS as service. Click "Connect Account" and sign in. OBS will pull your stream key automatically. You need 1,000 subscribers to go live on mobile YouTube, but zero requirements for desktop streaming.

03

🎬Build Your Scene

Create a scene with these sources: Game Capture (captures your game fullscreen) → Video Capture Device (your iPhone via Camo, set as a small overlay) → Audio Input (Lark M1 microphone) → Browser Source (for free overlays from sites like Nerd or Die).

⚠️ WiFi Warning
On WiFi, use a 5GHz band and stay close to your router. For a 6000 kbps stream you need at least 10 Mbps upload speed. Run a speedtest at fast.com — if upload is under 8 Mbps, drop bitrate to 4500 kbps and use 720p60 instead.
04 — Audio
LARK M1 SETUP
A

🎙Connection

The Lark M1 transmitter plugs into your laptop via USB-C or 3.5mm (check your model). In OBS, go to Settings → Audio and set the Mic/Auxiliary device to the Lark M1. It'll show up as "USB Audio" or "Hollyland Lark."

B

🎛OBS Audio Filters

Right-click your mic source in OBS → Filters → Add these in order: Noise Suppression (RNNoise method) → Noise Gate (close threshold -32dB, open -26dB) → Compressor (ratio 4:1) → Gain (boost if too quiet). This makes your voice sound professional.

C

🎧CloudX Headset

Use the CloudX for game audio monitoring only — don't use it as your stream mic. The Lark M1 is your star mic. In Windows Sound settings, set the CloudX as your default playback device and Lark M1 as your recording device.

06 — Before You Go Live
LAUNCH CHECKLIST
  • OBS test stream — Stream to YouTube as Unlisted for 10 min. Check for lag, audio sync, dropped frames.
  • Check upload speed — Must be 8 Mbps+ for 6000 kbps. Test at fast.com before every stream.
  • Plug in laptop — Never stream on battery. Performance throttles and temps spike.
  • Close background apps — Chrome, Discord (use overlay mode), Spotify. Free up RAM for OBS + game.
  • Set Windows power plan — Control Panel → Power Options → High Performance.
  • Enable NVENC in OBS — Settings → Output → Hardware (NVENC). This is your RTX 3050's superpower.
  • Set up stream title & thumbnail — Canva has free templates. A good title = discoverability.
  • Free overlay — streamlabs.com/library or nerdordie.com for free stream packages.
  • Discord server — Create a community space on day one. Even 5 members helps.
  • Posting schedule — Consistency > quality at the start. 3× per week minimum.
07 — Hardware Tips
KEEP IT COOL

🌡Thermal Management

Laptops throttle under sustained load (gaming + encoding). Keep temps in check:

  • → Use a laptop cooling pad (~$20) — makes a real difference
  • → Stream in Game Mode (Windows Settings → Gaming → Game Mode: On)
  • → Cap game FPS to 120 FPS max — no reason to render 300 FPS while streaming
  • → Monitor temps with HWiNFO64 — CPU should stay under 90°C
  • → If GPU memory is low, lower game settings slightly — RTX 3050 has 4GB VRAM
