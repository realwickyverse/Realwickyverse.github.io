📅 Consistency

BUILD YOURSCHEDULE

The most underrated growth tool is simply showing up on time, every time. Here's how to build a schedule you can actually stick to.

01 — The Plans
PICK YOUR LEVEL
Starter
3× / WEEK
Perfect if you have school, work, or other commitments. Sustainable long-term.
  • 2 streams (2–3 hrs each)
  • 1 YouTube video (highlights)
  • 3 Shorts from stream clips
  • Total: ~10 hrs/week
Growth
5× / WEEK
For those ready to push harder. Noticeable growth within 60–90 days.
  • 3 streams (2–3 hrs each)
  • 2 YouTube videos
  • 5 Shorts daily
  • Total: ~18 hrs/week
Full Grind
DAILY
Max output. Only sustainable short-term unless you love every second of it.
  • 5 streams/week
  • 3 YouTube videos/week
  • 1 Short every day
  • Total: ~25+ hrs/week
💡 Real Talk
Start with 3× per week. Most new streamers burn out within 2 months doing daily streams. Slow and consistent beats fast and inconsistent every time. YouTube rewards channels that post regularly for months — not channels that explode then disappear.

🕐 Best Times to Go Live on YouTube

Based on when gaming audiences are most active:

  • → Weekdays: 7pm–11pm local time — after work/school hours
  • → Weekends: 2pm–6pm — afternoon sessions get the most viewers
  • → Avoid: 10am–4pm weekdays — most of your audience is busy
  • → Consistency > perfect timing — same day + same time every week builds habit for your viewers
03 — Staying Consistent
AVOID BURNOUT
🎯
Set a Minimum
Decide on the minimum you'll do even on bad days — maybe just 1 hour or just posting one Short. Showing up badly beats not showing up.
📵
Take a Real Day Off
Pick one full day per week with zero content work. No editing, no streaming, no analytics checking. Rest makes the other days better.
📊
Don't Check Analytics Daily
Check once per week max. Daily analytics watching at 0–100 subscribers is a fast path to quitting. Give content 30–60 days to perform.
🏆
Celebrate Small Wins
First stream, first 10 subs, first comment from a stranger — these matter. Write them down. The early milestones are the ones you'll remember.

📅 Use a Content Calendar

Plan your stream topics and video ideas one week in advance. Free tools: Notion (free), Google Calendar, or even a paper notebook. Knowing what you're streaming next week removes the mental barrier of "what do I even do today?" — which is the #1 reason streamers skip sessions.

04 — The 90-Day Rule
COMMIT TO 90 DAYS

🗓 Why 90 Days Changes Everything

Most streamers quit within 30 days because they don't see results. Here's what actually happens on a consistent 3×/week schedule:

  • 30
    Days — Technical confidenceYour OBS setup is smooth, you stop worrying about settings, you start focusing on content.
  • 60
    Days — First real fansA few regulars who come back every stream. YouTube starts recommending your Shorts to new people.
  • 90
    Days — Algorithm tractionYouTube has enough data about your channel to start pushing it. This is when growth accelerates. Most people quit at day 29.
